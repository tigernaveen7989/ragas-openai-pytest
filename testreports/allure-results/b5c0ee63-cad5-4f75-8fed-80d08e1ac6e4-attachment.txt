### Chargeable Seats
If a seat is usually chargeable (e.g., for preferred seating in airlines) but offered at a discount (via promotions, loyalty points, or special deals), you pay the reduced fee to reserve it. This secures better positioning (like extra legroom) versus a standard, free assigned seat. Discounts are often temporary, so confirm at booking. If you decline, you get a complimentary assignment based on airline policy, potentially with less desirable spots (e.g., middle row).

### Chargeable Ancillaries
Ancillaries like extra baggage, meals, or priority boarding are optional add-ons with fees, which can be discounted (e.g., through coupons or memberships). You pay the lowered amount to add them; otherwise, they're not included. Discounts vary by provider and may require conditions like early booking.
